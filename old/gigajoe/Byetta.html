Byetta

The manufacturer of Byetta, the popular diabetes medication, failed to warn users of the risk of one of the worst cancers a person can acquire – pancreatic cancer. This very serious Byetta side effect has inflicted pain and suffering on people all over the country. Other potential Byetta side effects include nausea, hypoglycemia, vomiting, diarrhea, headache, nervousness, and stomach discomfort.

Diabetes Drugs and Pancreatic Cancer

Medications widely used to treat Type 2 diabetes including Byetta, Victoza, and Januvia have been identified in preliminary studies as potential triggers for pancreatitis and pancreatic cancer in humans. Patients who take these medications may also experience elevations in the level of serum calcitonin in their body, which is often a sign of thyroid cancer.

FDA Issues Warnings Concerning Byetta Side Effects

In 2008, the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) strengthened warnings about Byetta side effects including potentially life-threatening pancreas problems after receiving reports of deaths and other hospitalizations in Byetta users.

In addition, from April 2005 through October 2008, the FDA received 78 Byetta side effects reports of problems with kidney function in patients. Some cases occurred in patients with pre-existing kidney disease, while other cases occurred in patients with one or more risk factors for developing kidney problems. This prompted the FDA to mandate the makers of Byetta to revise their label to include safety information on possible kidney problems
