DDePuy LPS Diaphyseal Sleeve

According to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), the DePuy LPS Diaphyseal Sleeve’s taper connection to the device’s base may not be strong enough to support workloads caused by everyday physical activities. If the taper connection fractures after being implanted, severe or potentially life threatening injuries may result. If you or someone you love has been injured, you may be entitled to compensation. Contact My Vaccine Lawyer for a free legal consultation.

The DePuy LPS Diaphyseal Sleeve is a knee replacement device created by DePuy Orthopaedics, Inc., a division of Johnson & Johnson (J&J). The device is used to create a better fit and feel for patients receiving knee replacement with the company’s Limb Preservation System (LPS).

The problem with the LPS Sleeve is that certain connecting parts may not support potential loads during a patient’s normal activities, resulting in a fracture of the sleeve. Due to these defects, in January 2013, DePuy issued an urgent Medical Device Recall for their LPS sleeve. On February 15, 2013, the FDA issued a Class I recall—a warning issued when a medical device may cause severe injuries or death—after receiving numerous reports of the devices’ failure.

Complications from a fractured LPS Sleeve include:

  • Loss of function and mobility
  • Loss of limb
  • Infection
  • Soft tissue damage
  • Death

If you or a loved one suffered any injury due to implantation of the recalled DePuy LPS Diaphyseal Sleeve, you may be entitled to compensation. Contact My Vaccine Lawyer today for a free legal consultation and to see if a DePuy knee replacement lawsuit is appropriate for you.
