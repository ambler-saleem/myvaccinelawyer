Arleton Spacer

Since the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) approved the Artelon® CMC Spacer for sale, multiple patients and doctors have filed adverse event reports due to the products failure. These reports often involve an inflammatory response and swelling bone loss. More recently, recipients have reported device breakages in the hand. If you or someone you love has been injured by AlloDerm, you may be entitled to compensation. Contact My Vaccine Lawyer for a free legal consultation.

The Artelon® CMC Spacer is a medical device used to treat osteoarthritis in degenterative joints of the thumb. The T-shaped device, made of a woven textile called Artelon, is surgically implanted near the base of the thumb to help alleviate pain and give arthritis patients more mobility. The device is manufactured by Artimplant AB and marketed by Small Bone Innovations, Inc

The Artelon® CMC Spacer is said to be biocompatible and degradeble, however, several reports of adverse events have been received by the FDA, including failure of this device.

Problems reported to the FDA include:

  • Pain and inflamation
  • Bone loss
  • Redness at base of the thumb
  • Misshaped/oddly shaped thumb
  • Hand joint pain and swelling
  • Additional thumb surgeries
  • Limited range of motion
